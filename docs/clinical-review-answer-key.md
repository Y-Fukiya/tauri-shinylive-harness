# Clinical Review Exercise Answer Key

This answer key is for facilitator use. Exact values may change when synthetic
data packs are regenerated, so focus on the review pattern rather than a single
clinical conclusion.

## Exercise 1

Expected review behavior:

- The reviewer compares lab trend timing with AE start/end days.
- The reviewer confirms whether the event is plausibly treatment-emergent.
- The reviewer checks seriousness, relatedness, severity, and outcome.
- The reviewer confirms that reports include data pack ID, data pack hash, and
  the clinical-use limitation.

## Exercise 2

Expected review behavior:

- The reviewer identifies active exposure intervals.
- The reviewer compares AE timing to exposure timing.
- The reviewer checks whether dose modifications are visible enough for
  discussion.
- The reviewer does not infer real-world medical action from synthetic data.

## Exercise 3

Expected review behavior:

- The reviewer confirms that validation checks are understandable.
- The reviewer distinguishes demo data checks from production EDC/SDTM edit
  checks.
- The reviewer notes controlled terminology and timeline rules that would need
  formal governance in a regulated workflow.

## Exercise 4

Expected review behavior:

- The reviewer opens the human-readable evidence index first.
- The reviewer can find validation, security, reproducibility, report, and
  sign-off evidence.
- The reviewer understands that sign-off is a demo marker, not a regulated
  electronic signature.
